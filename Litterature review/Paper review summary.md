The **User Equilibrium Traffic Assignment Problem (UE-TAP)** is crucial for managing traffic and urban mobility.      <br> <br>                                            . Traditional methods (e.g., optimization-based algorithms) are <span style="color:rgb(255, 0, 0)"><b>computationally expensive</b></span>, <span style="color:rgb(255, 0, 0)">rely on <b>strict assumptions</b></span>, and <span style="color:rgb(255, 0, 0)"><b>struggle with scalability</b>.</span> | The authors aimed to **develop a new data-driven method** to solve the **User Equilibrium Traffic Assignment Problem (UE-TAP)** — that is, to estimate how traffic distributes itself in a network **when every traveler chooses the route that minimizes their own travel time** (i.e., the _user equilibrium_ condition) — <span style="color:rgb(255, 0, 0)"><b>without relying on traditional optimization or iterative algorithms</b></span>. | .
